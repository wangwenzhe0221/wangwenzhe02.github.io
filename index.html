01.

The 9th island

Site: Amatuku, Funafuti, Tuvalu

Tuvalu is the first country in the world to face the threat of submersion due to rising sea levels. A country without its own territory cannot be recognized as a sovereign state, thus Tuvaluans is facing an identity crisis. Our project aims to establish a data center to preserve data for Tuvalu and provide Tuvaluans with the opportunity to live in their homeland.

AIGC Conceptual Short Film: "Tuvalu Data Center"

[ Visual Storytelling ]

Directed a conceptual short film on Tuvalu's climate crisis. Translated the abstract ideas of "digital homeland" and "identity preservation" into an emotionally inspiring visual narrative and storyboard.

[ 3D-Guided AIGC Generation ]

Built 3D base models in Rhino/Blender for precise camera control. Leveraged Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, and Gemini to generate high-fidelity concept art, extensively tuning prompts to achieve a highly original "cyber-coral" futuristic aesthetic.

[ AI Video Production & Post-Editing ]

Leveraged Kling AI for complex text-to-video and image-to-video generation, bringing static narrative nodes to life. Finalized post-production using CapCut and Premiere Pro, delivering an AI-driven utopian cinematic film showcasing the architectural project.

Circular Fabrication Platform

[ Product Strategy ]

Designed a sustainable customization platform to address the scalability bottleneck in construction waste upcycling, achieving a digital closed-loop workflow from "waste material reception" to "furniture fabrication."

[ Database Design ]

Developed a "Material Passport" system and a web-based front-end, enabling users to seamlessly filter base materials across various databases via text prompts and visual selectors.

[ Core Algorithm: Prompt-to-Fabrication ]

Integrated Generative AI with underlying Grasshopper algorithms, establishing the key computational nodes:

AI Concept Generation → Voxelization → Greedy Box Fitting → Rule-driven Material Remapping

[ Project Impact ]

Bridged the gap between text prompts, 3D modeling, and physical manufacturing, delivering a high-conversion SaaS prototype for the circular economy.

03.

An Interactive Undersea Realm

Project Overview

"An Interactive Undersea Realm" is a web-based 3D configurator connecting back-end parametric logic (Grasshopper) with front-end interactive rendering (Three.js). It allows users to intuitively generate and manipulate 3D digital art in the browser, showcasing strong product thinking in UI/UX design and complex data integration.

Launch Web Application ↗
Parametric Generation: Translates web UI inputs into JSON data to drive complex 3D geometry generation via Grasshopper and the Warp plugin.
Immersive Environment: Uses custom CSS, parallax animations, and advanced Three.js materials to create a high-quality, visually engaging underwater aesthetic.
Direct 3D Manipulation: Empowers users to select, move, rotate, and scale individual 3D objects directly within the browser using intuitive transform controls.
Clean Interface: Features a clean, decluttered interface where users can easily show or hide control panels to maintain visual immersion.

Architecture Project 01: Saffron Horizon

Category: Architectural Design

This project is located in the Bamiyan region of Afghanistan, where women have long faced disempowerment and social marginalization. By establishing a modular community centered on saffron cultivation, the project aims to empower women economically and socially while offering a sustainable, high-value alternative to the widespread cultivation of opium. The design integrates local architectural typology and sociocultural context to meet agricultural production needs while promoting women's empowerment. By combining modular housing with functional saffron workshops, the project creates shared courtyards and public spaces that encourage inclusivity and collaboration.

Architecture Project 02: Echoes of Jiangnan

Category: Architectural Design

Zhonglou Tou is a historic urban village located in a bustling area, yet it is profoundly disconnected from the surrounding modern urban environment. The community facilities here are outdated, and the majority of the residents are elderly, with significant economic segregation. This project emphasizes that public spaces should act as neutral social platforms, fostering encounters between people from diverse backgrounds. By translating Jiangnan traditional painting, the design creates a series of scenes that capture the essence of the region. The cultural community center, which includes a theater, tea room, and exhibition hall, is designed to promote social interaction among the elderly while also attracting younger generations.

Architecture Project 03: Mind Garden

Category: Architectural Design / Art Therapy Center

The project is an art therapy center designed for adolescents aged 10-18 with depression. Using the serotonin pathway as the design framework for the architectural layout, the project combines art therapy with modern medical practices to provide a safe and supportive environment. It aims to help adolescents engage in non-verbal emotional expression and self-exploration. By fostering creativity, encouraging positive thinking, and cultivating self-awareness, the project strives to heal psychological trauma while uncovering and nurturing their personal talents and potential.
